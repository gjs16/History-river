0. The Shared Source (c. 30–100)

What’s happening: Jesus’ life, death, and resurrection. Apostles preach and plant churches around the Mediterranean. No denominations yet.

Core shared beliefs almost all Christians still hold:

  • Jesus is Lord and Messiah: Acts 2:36; Romans 10:9–13.
  • Salvation by grace through faith: Ephesians 2:8–9.
  • Jesus died for our sins and rose: 1 Corinthians 15:1–4.
  • The Trinity (Father/Son/Spirit divine): Matthew 28:19; John 1:1–14.
  • Call to love and holiness: John 13:34–35; Galatians 5:22–26.

This is the deep “underground aquifer” almost all later Christians still drink from.

1. Early Councils & Creeds (Nicaea)

Big Question: Is Jesus fully God, or a lesser created being?

Side A: "Pro-Nicene" (Became mainstream)

Goal: Protect the worship of Jesus as truly God; guard salvation as God’s own work.

Emphasis: The Son is "of one substance" with the Father.

Verses cited:
John 1:1–3, 14
John 20:28
Colossians 2:9
Hebrews 1:3

Side B: Arian views

Goal: Guard God’s oneness; avoid saying there are "two Gods."

Emphasis: Son is the first and greatest creation, exalted but not equal.

Verses cited:
John 14:28
Colossians 1:15
Proverbs 8:22–25

Result: The Nicene Creed affirms Jesus is fully God and fully man.

2. The Great Schism (1054) – East & West

Big Question: Authority & The Pope

West (Rome/Catholic): Papal Primacy

Goal: Preserve unity and provide a final universal court of appeal.

Emphasis: Peter’s special role continues specifically in Rome’s bishop (the Pope).

Verses cited:
Matthew 16:18–19 ("You are Peter... keys of the kingdom")
Luke 22:31–32
John 21:15–17

East (Orthodox): Conciliarity

Goal: Preserve shared leadership among bishops; avoid one dominating the others.

Emphasis: All apostles are foundations; local bishops decide together in council.

Verses cited:
Ephesians 2:19–22 (built on "apostles and prophets")
Acts 15 (The Jerusalem Council)
1 Peter 5:1–3

3. The Reformation (1500s)

The river splits significantly here. The debate centered on salvation, authority, and sacraments.

Big Question: How are we saved? (Justification)

Protestant Reformers (Luther/Calvin)

Goal: Make clear that salvation is entirely God’s gift, not earned.

Emphasis: Justification by grace alone through faith alone. Good works are fruit, not root.

Verses cited:
Ephesians 2:8–9
Romans 3:23–28
Romans 4:4–5
Galatians 2:16

Roman Catholic (Council of Trent)

Goal: Protect the idea that grace must change us and obedience matters.

Emphasis: Justified by grace through faith, but faith must be "formed by love" (works) and cooperate with grace.

Verses cited:
James 2:14–26 ("faith without works is dead")
Matthew 7:21
Romans 2:6–7
